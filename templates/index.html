The Humble Developer

and Why He Should Join Humble Bundle

He believes. He believes in the awesomeness and potential more awesomeness of Humble Bundle. There is a reason Humble Bundle is fun, and a reason that purchasing a Bundle gives you a warm fuzzy feeling inside. It's because Humble Bundle provides a legitimate way for users to pay for what we want, in the way we want it. There's no hassle of DRM. There's no system or vendor lock-in. There's no buyer's remorse because one of the games wasn't what you'd hoped it would be—"The other games were still great, plus I supported charity!" There's no guilt for pirating. There's no worry about losing your copy of the game. There's just the stuff we like and the chance to support its creators, so they'll make more stuff we like.

And he gets it. He gets it like Humble Bundle really gets it. There are other sites following HB's lead, offering indie bundles, and their existence is most welcome. Their existence provides friendly competition, which leads to innovation, and their existence further validates the market. But they don't seem to entirely get it—not like Humble Bundle does. Cross-platform, 100% DRM-free, pay-only-what-you-want, always-available entertainment. It is the future.
